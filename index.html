The News Feed - Part 1

  • Released to help keep users up-to-date on updates to their friends’ profiles
  • Announcement makes it clear that the news feed doesn’t show any more information than was already exposed
    • This doesn’t stop a backlash from Facebook users
    • Large protest groups are formed
  • Privacy controls existed, but were possibly too complex for some users

The News Feed - Part 2

  • Eventually forced an open letter response from CEO Mark Zuckerberg:
    • Facebook claims responsibility for not having created the proper privacy settings and creates a simpler privacy control panel
    • Zuckerberg reminds users that the News Feed allowed protest groups to be highlighted
  • Outcome: News Feed never goes away

Beacon - 2007

  • Automatic publishing of any purchases to the News Feed
    • Users petitioned gaining the ability to approve of the publishing before it was done
  • Originally not allowed to Opt-in or Opt-out
  • Doesn’t ask for user permission
  • Eventually allowed Opt-out and permission
  • 9.5 million dollar settlement fund
  • Shutdown September, 2009
  • 1x1 GIF web bug and Facebook Cookies
  • Log out and clear cookies

Instant Personalization - 2010

  • Allowed Partner websites to automatically plug in to a user’s news feed to allow a more personal experience
    • Triggers when a user goes to the partner’s site and is shown on the feed/advertising
    • Shown as a way to benefit from friend’s suggestions rather than strangers
  • Outcome: Automatic opt-in though you have to turn it off on every website

Like Button Plug-in - 2010

  • Used to show what the user likes on their profile.
    • This feature is designed to cater to the user on what they enjoy and do on the Internet and connect that to Facebook when they want.
  • “Like” button serves as a cookie and also tracks browsing history
  • Third-Party websites have access
  • Facebook claims to hold tracking data for 90 days
    • Date, IP, URL, time
  • “Share” button functions similarly

Facebook Recognition - 2012

  • Enabled on accounts without users consent
  • Used to automatically suggest “tagging” a person in a photo
    • Semi-automated tagging versus manual tagging
  • No ability to opt-out before implementation

Psychological Testing - 2012

  • 700,000 users
    • Group A - positive news feeds
    • Group B - negative news feeds
  • No “consent form” until four months after
    • No age limit - users under the age of 18
  • Are you emotionally impacted by your friend’s news feeds?
